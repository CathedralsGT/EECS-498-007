## Computer Vision & Deep Learning
Both are the subfield of AI (intelligent systems), while deep learning is also a subfield of machine learning.

### History of CV
*Hubel and Wiesel, 1959*:  measured the brain activity of cats, observed that simple cells response to **light orientation**, complex cells response to not only light orientation but also **movement**, hypercomplex cells can response to **movement with an end point**.
> What's important: the shift from **orients** to **edges** & the hierachical representation of the visual system

*Larry Roberts, 1963*: **edge detection**, get feature points to understand the 3D geometry of the objects in the image

*David Marr, 1970s*: proposed the concept of **stages of visual representation** (input image -> primal sketch -> 2 $\frac{1}{2}$-D sketch -> 3-D model representation)

*Recognition via Edge Detection, 1980s*: John Canny *Robots algorithm(1986)*, David Lowe *matching edges(1987)*

*Recognition via Grouping, 1990s*

*Recognition via Matching, 2000s*: template matching, by vector manipulation

*Viola and Jones, 2001*: face detection, using boosting decision tree

*IMAGENET*: large scale visual recognition challenge, a very influential dataset

*Krizhevsky et al, **Alexnet**, 2012*: deep learning entered
